History Defines the Future Bio

As a NEIU graduate student, I am transitioning from clinical mental health counseling into geography and environmental sciences. While honing my skills in GIS programs and software I use assignments as opportunities to enhance my understanding of geomorphology, archaeology, and paleographic reconstruction. My goal is to use GIS in answering some of the questions about geography before the first primates, some 65,000,000 years ago.

Over the 2016 summer, I volunteered with the Illinois State Archaeology Survey of the Prairie Research Institute doing shovel tests, soil identification, GPS tracking, and manual soil sifting. It was during this experience when I was introduced to the power of GIS. Using ArcMap and digitized hand-drawn maps, artifacts where traced back through time, through the various land owners and natural disasters. These maps embodied the power of visuals, changing mechanical dates and texts into simulating, graphic descriptions of the past. I’d like to use these visuals to help others understand our planet’s history.
